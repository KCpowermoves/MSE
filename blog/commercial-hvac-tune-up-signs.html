5 Signs Your Commercial RTU Needs a Tune-Up — Not a Replacement

December 3, 2025 6 min read

Your rooftop unit is struggling. The building isn't cooling evenly, the energy bills are up, and your maintenance vendor just quoted you $12,000 to $18,000 for a new unit. Before you sign that purchase order, it's worth asking a critical question: does this system actually need to be replaced, or does it need a tune-up?

The answer matters more than most business owners realize. A poorly maintained RTU can exhibit every symptom of a failing system — but respond dramatically to a professional tune-up. We've seen systems that were days away from a "replacement quote" deliver years of reliable service after a thorough tune-up. And under EmPOWER Maryland's commercial HVAC programs, that tune-up can be substantially covered by utility rebates.

Here are the five most common signs that your commercial RTU is a tune-up candidate, not a replacement candidate.

01
Uneven Cooling or Hot Spots in the Building
If some areas of your building are too hot while others are comfortable, the instinct is often to blame the equipment size or age. But in most cases, uneven cooling is a symptom of airflow and refrigerant charge issues — both of which are addressed in a standard tune-up. Dirty evaporator coils reduce heat transfer efficiency. Low refrigerant charge causes the system to work harder without delivering adequate cooling. Both issues are correctable without equipment replacement. A tune-up that includes coil cleaning and refrigerant charge verification can restore even cooling in a system that appears to be failing.
02
Unusually High Energy Bills With No Clear Cause
A 10–20% increase in energy costs that can't be explained by utility rate changes or occupancy shifts is often a sign of HVAC inefficiency. When an RTU is running with dirty coils, incorrect refrigerant charge, or improperly calibrated controls, it works much harder than it should to achieve the same cooling effect. The system logs more run hours, draws more power, and delivers less comfort. This is the "performance gap" that a tune-up closes. Industry data and utility program research suggest commercial HVAC tune-ups typically reduce HVAC energy consumption by 5–20%. For a 10,000 sq ft office building, that can translate to $100–$400/month in savings.
03
The System Short-Cycles (Turns On and Off Frequently)
Short-cycling — where the unit turns on, runs briefly, and shuts off again repeatedly — is a classic sign of a refrigerant issue or a controls problem. Both are addressable in a tune-up. Short-cycling is also hard on compressors and other mechanical components because startup draws significantly more current than steady-state operation. A system that's short-cycling is wearing itself out faster than it should, and replacing it before addressing the root cause means the new system will likely develop the same problem. Get the tune-up first.
04
The System Is 7–15 Years Old and Has Never Been Professionally Serviced
Commercial RTUs have a typical lifespan of 15–20 years when properly maintained. A system in the 7–15 year range that has never received a professional tune-up is likely operating well below its original efficiency rating — but it has plenty of useful life remaining. Think of it like a car with 80,000 miles that's never had an oil change: it's running rough, but it doesn't need to be replaced, it needs maintenance. EmPOWER Maryland's HVAC tune-up program specifically targets commercial systems that are at least 2 years old, making your mid-age RTU an ideal candidate.
05
You've Received Multiple Repair Calls, But No Diagnosis of Root Cause
If you've had service technicians out multiple times in the past year — replacing a contactor here, adding refrigerant there, replacing a capacitor — without a thorough diagnosis of why these parts keep failing, you may be in a reactive maintenance cycle. A comprehensive tune-up takes a systematic approach: checking refrigerant charge against manufacturer specs, measuring supply and return air temperatures, testing all electrical components, and calibrating controls. This diagnostic view often reveals that what looked like multiple component failures was actually a single underlying issue — and that replacement isn't needed.

When Replacement IS the Right Call

To be fair, some situations genuinely warrant replacement rather than a tune-up:

  • Compressor failure on an old system: Replacing a compressor on a system over 15 years old often doesn't make economic sense. A new, high-efficiency unit may deliver better ROI.
  • Refrigerant issues on R-22 systems: Systems using R-22 refrigerant face escalating refrigerant costs since R-22 production was phased out. If your R-22 system has a refrigerant leak, replacement is often the more cost-effective path.
  • Systems under 2 years old: New systems experiencing problems should be addressed under warranty, not with a tune-up service.

The Smart Approach: Get a professional assessment before deciding. A qualified technician can tell you within 30 minutes whether a system is a tune-up candidate or a genuine replacement candidate — and that assessment costs far less than an unnecessary replacement.
